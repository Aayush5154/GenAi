Sunlit pitch, the willow sings, leather arcs like fate.
A hush, then thunder: pads and breath collide.
Batsmen weave patience into golden waits,
fielders hold the sky in focused stride.
Sweat maps vows on collars, gloves, and brow;
the bowler's seam carves stories in the dirt.
Night floods lamps that crown the final hour,
crowds exhale when boundaries flirt.
Spin learns secrets from a trembling seam,
while slips count heartbeats between the creak.
Victory tastes of tea and shared dream;
a handshake seals respect, not streak.
Cricket: a gentled war of skill and grace,
where time keeps score on faces.
